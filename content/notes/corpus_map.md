---

_This project develops a process-first account of being, evaluation,
and non-executability. The essays were not written in final conceptual
order, so this page gives the structural order of the work as it
currently stands._

---


## Running Comes First {#running-comes-first}

This essay is the ontology correction. It argues that the primitive is
not invariance, consistency, or continuity treated as static conditions,
but self-sustaining process. Stability, distinction, and evaluation are
better understood as different appearances or resolutions of that
running.

This is now the best entry point to the project, because it corrects
the earlier invariance-first framing used in several of the essays
below.


## Role-Arity and the Structure of Emergence {#role-arity}

This essay introduces the diagnostic framework. It argues that questions
and operations require different numbers of functional roles, and that
predictable failure modes arise when the structure of a question does
not match the structure of its target. Overspecification produces
phantom depth. Underspecification produces false dichotomy.

This is the main diagnostic tool in the corpus.

_Note: this essay predates Running Comes First and should now be read in
light of its process-first correction. The diagnostic claims remain
intact; the ontological framing has since been sharpened._


## Why Is There Something Rather Than Nothing {#why-something}

This essay applies the diagnostic to a canonical metaphysical question.
It argues that the question is structurally malformed rather than merely
difficult or unanswered.

This is the first major philosophical application of the framework.

_Note: this essay uses the earlier invariance-first language and is best
read historically alongside Running Comes First._


## Consistency Gate {#consistency-gate}

This essay introduces V₁, the first boundary validator. It argues that
contradiction should be blocked at the boundary rather than patched
after the fact by weakening the inferential engine. On this view,
explosion is not a deep truth about logic but the result of allowing
substrate-canceling input to reach the engine.

This is the first formal boundary mechanism in the project.


## Executability Gate {#executability-gate}

This essay introduces V₂ and completes the validator architecture. It
treats contradiction and cyclic anchor-failure as two species of
non-executable input and specifies a typed gate over evaluative
requests. The essay applies the framework to logical explosion,
liar-style self-reference, the halting result, and Gödelian
incompleteness.

This is the main unification essay in the corpus.

---

Suggested reading order: start with _Running Comes First_, then
_Role-Arity and the Structure of Emergence_, then _Why Is There
Something Rather Than Nothing_, then _Consistency Gate_, and finally
_Executability Gate_.

Taken together, the essays argue for a process-first ontology, a
structural diagnostic for malformed questions, and a two-part boundary
architecture for catching non-executable input before it is mistaken
for a deep limit of logic, computation, or mathematics.
